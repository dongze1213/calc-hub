Whether you are building an emergency fund, saving for a down payment, or planning for a major purchase, this tool gives you a clear picture of your financial future. Simply enter your initial deposit, monthly contribution, interest rate, and time period to see your projected savings growth.

Features

  • See exactly how much of your total savings comes from interest versus your own deposits
  • 100% client-side — your financial data never leaves your browser

How Does Compound Interest Work for Savings?

Compound interest means you earn interest on both your original deposit and on previously earned interest. The more frequently interest is compounded, the faster your savings grow. This calculator uses monthly compounding, which is standard for most savings accounts. Over time, the compounding effect becomes more powerful — your interest starts earning interest, creating a snowball effect that accelerates your savings growth significantly compared to simple interest.

Savings Calculator FAQ

How much should I have in emergency savings?

Build 3-6 months of living expenses as an emergency fund. Keep it in a high-yield savings account for easy access. Start small and build consistently.

What is a high-yield savings account?

Online banks offer 4-5% APY vs traditional banks at 0.01-0.5%. High-yield accounts are FDIC insured and have no investment risk, making them ideal for emergency funds and short-term goals.

How does compound interest grow my savings?

Interest earns more interest. $1,000 at 5% becomes $1,050 after year one, then earns 5% on $1,050 in year two ($1,102.50), and so on. Over decades, this exponential growth is powerful.

Should I save or invest extra money?

Save for short-term goals (under 3-5 years) and emergencies. Invest for long-term goals (5+ years) to outpace inflation. Keep your emergency fund separate from investments.
