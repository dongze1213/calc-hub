About Salary Calculator

Our free salary calculator helps you estimate your take-home pay after federal and state taxes. Enter your annual salary, select your filing status, and see exactly how much of your gross income you get to keep. This tool uses 2024 US federal income tax brackets and standard FICA tax rates to provide an accurate paycheck estimate.

Features

  • Estimates take-home pay based on 2024 federal tax brackets
  • Supports Single, Married Filing Jointly, and Head of Household filing statuses
  • Shows pay per period — annual, monthly, bi-weekly, or weekly
  • 100% client-side — your salary data never leaves your browser

How Are Paycheck Taxes Calculated?

Your take-home pay is calculated by subtracting several taxes from your gross income. Federal income tax uses a progressive bracket system — each portion of your income is taxed at a different rate. For 2024, the brackets for Single filers are 10% on income up to $11,600, 12% up to $47,150, 22% up to $100,525, 24% up to $191,950, 32% up to $243,725, 35% up to $609,350, and 37% on income above that. FICA taxes include Social Security at 6.2% on income up to $168,600 and Medicare at 1.45% on all income (plus an additional 0.9% on income above $200,000 for Single filers). State income taxes vary widely by state; this calculator uses an average flat rate of 4.6%. Pre-tax deductions like 401(k) contributions and HSA contributions reduce your taxable income, which can lower your overall tax burden.
