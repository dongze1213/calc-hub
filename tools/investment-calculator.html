About Investment Calculator

What is an Investment Calculator?

An investment calculator is a powerful financial tool that helps you project how your money will grow over time. Whether you're saving for retirement, a major purchase, or building wealth, understanding compound interest and regular contributions is essential for achieving your financial goals.

Our free investment calculator uses the time-tested compound interest formula to show you exactly how your initial investment and monthly contributions will grow. The power of compound interest means your money earns returns, and those returns earn their own returns, creating exponential growth over time.

How to Use This Calculator

Simply enter your initial investment amount, how much you plan to contribute monthly, your expected annual return rate, and how long you intend to invest. The calculator will instantly show you your projected future value, total contributions, and the investment returns earned through compound interest.

The Investment Formula

The calculator uses the future value of an annuity formula: FV = P(1+r)^n + PMT×[((1+r)^n - 1)/r], where P is your initial investment, r is the monthly interest rate, n is the total number of months, and PMT is your monthly contribution. This formula accounts for compound interest on both your initial investment and your recurring contributions.

Investment Planning Tips

  • Start early: The earlier you begin investing, the more time your money has to compound.
  • Be consistent: Regular monthly contributions, even small ones, add up significantly over time.
  • Diversify: Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Spread investments across different asset classes.
  • Review regularly: Reassess your investments periodically to ensure they align with your goals.
  • Consider inflation: Remember that inflation reduces purchasing power over long periods.

Benefits of Compound Interest

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest the eighth wonder of the world. With an 8% annual return, your money doubles approximately every 9 years. Our calculator shows you the dramatic difference between your total contributions and your actual returns, highlighting the wealth-building power of compound interest.

Investment Calculator FAQ

How much should I invest each month?

Aim for 15-20% of gross income for retirement. Start with whatever you can afford and increase 1% yearly. Even $100/month at 7% grows to over $120,000 in 30 years.

What is a realistic expected rate of return?

The S&P 500 has historically averaged about 10% annually (7% after inflation). A diversified portfolio might realistically expect 6-8% long-term returns before inflation.

What is dollar-cost averaging?

Investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market conditions. You buy more shares when prices are low and fewer when high, reducing your average cost per share over time.

When is the best time to start investing?

As early as possible. Time in the market beats timing the market. Starting 10 years earlier with half the monthly contribution often results in more total wealth due to compound growth.
